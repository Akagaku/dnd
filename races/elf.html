Elf

Magical and foreign, yet strange and alien at the same time, elves are a race of people descended from a group known simply as the Twelve: twelve human warriors experimented on by unseelie fey. They were engineered as living weapons to be used against the dragons, and stripped of their less useful instincts—emotion, empathy, and humanity—in exchange for wisdom and long life. Though once the ruling class of a powerful empire, elves have since been dethroned, and even relegated to the role of second rate citizens in some parts of the world.

Uncanny Visage

When the first elves were created, their fey influence manifested in eerie, slightly inhuman ways. They were thin and unnaturally tall, and all pigment was drained from their hair, skin, and eyes, making them frighteningly pale. Though this is still true of many moon elves, these features become more diluted each generation, preserved only by the elves' long lives and slow breeding cycle. Modern elves are often more similar in color to humans than to their ancestors.

Though few mind such superficial changes, with their feylike features fades their magical nature, leading the ancient moon elves to depart the material plane for the Feywild in an attempt to recapture their supernatural properties. The Alkarian sun elves are an example of the state of decay affecting elf lineage, harboring little if any connection to the Feywild and elven customs.

The Bones of an Empire

The end of the Age of Dragons saw the rise of the Age of the Elves. For thousands of years, the kingdom of Sothon ruled over most of the northern isle, and their reach included even the desert region of Alkaria on southern isle. Today, the isles are blanketed with Sothonian ruins. Several families of moon elves still cling desperately to the remnants of their fallen houses, tracking lineages and collecting heirlooms.

Subrace

There are two subraces of elf: Sothonian and Alkarian. Choose one of the two subraces before creating your character. You gain all of the traits listed under the Elf Traits section, as well as those listed under your chosen subrace.

Elf Traits

Your elf character has a variety of natural abilities, a result of fey magic and millennia of elven tradition.

  • Ability Score Increase. Your Wisdom score increases by 1.
  • Age. Elves do not reach physical maturity until the age of 30, and generally aren't considered adults until the age of 100, though Sothonian children are deemed combat ready as soon as they can swing a blade.
  • Speed. Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
  • Trance. Elves don't need to sleep. Instead, they enter a trance-like state for 4 hours a day. After resting in this way, you gain the benefit of 8 hours of sleep.

Sothonian

Also called moon elves, Sothonians are the children of Sothon, the first city of the elven dominion founded by and named for the warrior Sothos. They have historically had stark white hair, skin, and eyes. Over the centuries, however, the blood of the feytouched has thinned, and gray-eyed elves with more humanlike hair and skin are not uncommon.

Sothonian tradition is particularly warlike. Gladiators fight for the entertainment of the masses, and feats of strength are ritually performed on holidays. However, they also value strength of mind as much as strength of arm. Many advances in philosphy were made by Sothonian scholars, and Sothon was home to the most imporant scientific institutions of its age.

  • Ability Score Increase. Either your Strength score or Dexterity score increases by 2.
  • Size. Sothonians tower above both humans and Alkarians, standing between 6 and 7 feet tall. Your size is Medium.
  • Arms of Evrys. You have proficiency with the shortsword, shortbow, spear, and shield. The armor class bonus provided by your shield is increased by 1 while adjacent to at least one ally.
  • Philosopher Kings. You have proficiency with two skills of your choice from History, Insight, Medicine, Nature, and Religion.
  • Dragonslayer. You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened.
  • Languages. You can speak, read, and write Tenian and Sothonian.

Alkarian

At the height of its power, Sothon, led by Evrys the Indomitable, extended its reach across the Wound to a desert known to the deserts of Alkaria. In an ironic twist of fate, the fall of Sothon left the Alkarian colony as the only independent elven nation for many centuries. Millennia of interbreeding with the native humans resulted in a strain of elf known as sun elves, shorter and darker of hair and skin, and green or brown of eye.

Due to their admiration for the culture of their human ancestors, the sun elves aren't nearly as aggerssive as the children of Sothon, but are no less shrewd. They are merchants and traders, scholars and mathematicians, wise men and sorcerers. Harazal, the nocturnal City of Gold, is the capital of Alkaria, and has made its name as one of the leading economic powers in the world.

  • Ability Score Increase. Either your Constitution score or Charisma score increases by 2.
  • Wanderer. You are more resistant to hot climates, and can go twice as long without food or water before suffering from exhaustion.
  • Darkvision. You can see in dim light within 60 feet as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern colors in darkness, only shades of gray.
  • Desert Sorcery. You know the Prestidigitation cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you can cast Charm Person once as a 1st-level spell, using Charisma as your spellcasting ability. You must finish a long rest to cast it again with this trait.
  • Languages. You can speak, read, and write Tenian and Alkarian.
