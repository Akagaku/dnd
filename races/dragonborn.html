Dragonborn

Before the days of men and elves and dwarves, and before the Wound parted the Isles, the land and skies were ruled by an empire of dragons. Few yet live, if any, but the legacy of the mighty wyrms survives through the dragonborn. Seeking to usurp the dragons' power, ancient human shamans conducted foul rituals using the blood of those slain by the first elves, twisting themselves into the humanoid reptiles that are now commonplace throughout the world. Metallic dragonborn are rare, gem dragonborn even more so.

Subrace

Dragonborn have different traits depending on their draconic heritage, resulting in three subraces: chromatic, metallic, and gem. Choose one of the three subraces before creating your character. You gain all of the traits listed under the Dragonborn Traits section, as well as those listed under your chosen subrace.

Dragonborn Traits

  • Ability Score Increase. Your Strength score increases by 2.
  • Age. Young dragonborn grow quickly. They walk hours after hatching, and reach adulthood by 12. They live to be around 60.
  • Size. Dragonborn are taller and heavier than humans, standing well over 6 feet tall and averaging almost 250 pounds. Your size is Medium.
  • Speed. Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
  • Damage Resistance. You have resistance to the damage type associated with your draconic ancestry (specified in the tables below).
  • Breath Weapon. When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can replace one of your attacks with a breath attack. Each creature in the attack's area must make a Dexterity saving throw (DC = 8 + your Constitution modifier + your proficiency bonus). On a failed save, the creature takes 1d10 damage of the type associated with your draconic ancestry. On a successful save, it takes half as much damage. This damage increases by 1d10 when you reach 5th level (2d10), 11th level (3d10), and 17th level (4d10). You can use your Breath Weapon a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Chromatic

These dragonborn are descended from the chromatic dragons of old who rose up against their metallic fathers, leading to the civil war that would spark the downfall of dragonkind. Their inherent immunity to dragon breath attacks made them adept kinslayers, and their might and number drove even the proud dwarves to seal themselves away under the earth. Though they are the most common dragonborn, they can't seem to escape the shadow of their malevolent forebears, and are sometimes hunted and killed for sport. Their scales are colorful but dull, a pale reflection of the metallic dragons of old.

  • Ability Score Increase. Your Constitution score increases by 1.
  • Chromatic Ancestry. Your draconic ancestry is based on one of the dragon types from the table below.
Dragon Damage Type Breath Weapon
Black Acid 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex save)
Blue Lightning 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex save)
Green Poison 15 ft. cone (Con save)
Red Fire 15 ft. cone (Dex save)
White Cold 15 ft. cone (Con save)
  • Hide of the Usurpers. As an action, you can channel your draconic energy to protect yourself. For 1 minute, you are immune to the damage type associated with your ancestry. Once you use this trait, you can't do so again until you finish a long rest.

Metallic

Heirs to the blood of the metallic lords, metallic dragonborn are the closest things living to the children of Bahamut. As the metallic dragons had already suffered tremendous losses by the time dragon blood sorcery was discovered by humans, metallic dragonborn are somewhat of a rare sight. Many still see them as mythological symbols of virtue, living remnants of an ancient good long thought destroyed. Their bodies are coated in brilliant scales of gleaming metal, which shine in the sunlight.

  • Ability Score Increase. Your Charisma score increases by 1.
  • Metallic Ancestry. Your draconic ancestry is based on one of the dragon types from the table below.
Dragon Damage Type Breath Weapon
Brass Fire 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex save)
Bronze Lightning 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex save)
Copper Avid 5 by 30 ft. line (Dex save)
Gold Fire 15 ft. cone (Dex save)
Silver Cold 15 ft. cone (Con save)
  • Breath of Bahamut. You have a second breath weapon. When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can replace one of your attacks with an exhalation in a 15-foot cone. The save DC for this breath is 8 + your Constitution modifier + your proficiency bonus. Whenever you use this trait, choose one of the following options:
    • Enervating Breath. Each creature in the cone must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or become incapacitated until the start of your next turn.
    • Repulsion Breath. Each creature in the cone must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be pushed 20 feet away from you and be knocked prone.

Gem

When the civil war began, some younger metallic dragons fled their homes out of fear, tunneling deep into the earth and ever after living in gem-filled caverns in the deep. Over the course of thousands of years, they adapted to the psionic energy emitted by the gemstones, and were slowly transformed in a process similar to that which created the gem dwarves of Despin. Knowledge of the gem dragons is scant even among the most learned of scholars, and, as such, gem dragonborn are incredibly rare. Unlike other dragons, gem dragons and gem dragonborn have no scales. Instead, their skin is hard, smooth, and polished. Broken pieces of their horns and bodies float diligently in their places due to their inherent psionic power.

  • Ability Score Increase. Your Intelligence score increases by 1.
  • Gemstone Ancestry. Your draconic ancestry is based on one of the dragon types from the table below.
Dragon Damage Type Breath Weapon
Amethyst Force 15 ft. cone (Dex save)
Crystal Radiant 15 ft. cone (Dex save)
Emerald Psychic 15 ft. cone (Dex save)
Sapphire Thunder 15 ft. cone (Dex save)
Topaz Necrotic 15 ft. cone (Dex save)
  • Psionic Mind. You can telepathically speak to any creature you can see within 30 feet of you. You don't need to share a language with the creature, but the creature must be able to understand at least one language.
  • Gem Flight. As a bonus action, you gain a flying speed equal to your walking speed and can hover. Once you use this trait, you can't do so again until you finish a long rest.
  • Sunlight Sensitivity. While in sunlight, you have disadvantage on Attack rolls, as well as on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.
