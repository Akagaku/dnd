Dragonborn

Before the days of men and elves and dwarves, and before the Wound parted the Isles, the land and skies were ruled by a nation of dragons. Few yet live, if any, but the legacy of the mighty wyrms survives through the dragonborn. Seeking to usurp the dragons' power, ancient human shamans conducted foul rituals using the blood of those slain by the first elves, twisting themselves into the humanoid reptiles that are now commonplace throughout the world. Metallic dragonborn are rare, gem dragonborn even more so.

Heirs of the Dragons

The dragon children of Bahamut presided over the world for untold millennia before even the dwarves came to be. Their reign, however, came to an end when the evil dragon god Tiamat, whom Bahamut imprisoned beneath his palace, escaped her bonds with the help of unseelie fey tricksters. The two engaged in the Battle of the Drake Lords, the first battle of the War of the Usurpers. In a draw, both plummeted to the earth below, wounding the isles.

The remaining dragons were thrust into bloody civil war, becoming easy prey for the elves, who wiped nearly every one of them out. Today, whatever dragons survived the war are in hiding, while dragonborn have become commonplace throughout the world.

Dark and Twisted Sorcery

An early human shaman discovered the secrets of an ancient blood ritual now lost to time. Using the blood of the dragons who died in the War of the Usurpers, he turned his followers into the first dragonborn. They gained immeasurable strength and took on the aspect of the dragon, at the cost of their lifespan. It is said that the shaman himself imbibed the blood of Tiamat, and lurks among the mountains and caves of the world to this day.

Subrace

Dragonborn have different traits depending on their draconic heritage, resulting in three subraces: chromatic, metallic, and gem. Choose one of the three subraces before creating your character. You gain all of the traits listed under the Dragonborn Traits section, as well as those listed under your chosen subrace.

Dragonborn Traits

The power of the dragons' blood grants dragonborn a number of inehrent supernatural abilities.

  • Ability Score Increase. Your Strength score increases by 2.
  • Age. Young dragonborn grow quickly. They walk hours after hatching, and reach adulthood by 10. They live to be around 40.
  • Size. Dragonborn are taller and heavier than humans, standing well over 6 feet tall and averaging almost 250 pounds. Your size is Medium.
  • Speed. Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
  • Damage Resistance. You have resistance to the damage type associated with your draconic ancestry (determined by your subrace).
  • Breath Weapon. When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can replace one of your attacks with a breath attack. Each creature in the attack's area must make a Dexterity saving throw (DC = 8 + your Constitution modifier + your proficiency bonus). On a failed save, the creature takes 1d10 damage of the type associated with your draconic ancestry. On a successful save, it takes half as much damage. This damage increases by 1d10 when you reach 5th level (2d10), 11th level (3d10), and 17th level (4d10). You can use your Breath Weapon a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Chromatic

These dragonborn are descended from the chromatic dragons who rose up against their metallic fathers, leading to the war that sparked the downfall of dragonkind. Their immunity to dragon breath attacks made them adept kinslayers, and their might and number drove even the proud dwarves to hide under the earth.

Though they are the most common type of dragonborn, they can't seem to escape the shadow of their malevolent forebears, and are sometimes killed for sport by moon elves. Their scales are colorful but dull, a pale reflection of the metallic dragons of old.

Metallic

Heirs to the blood of the platinum, these dragonborn are the closest known living things to the descendants of Bahamut. As the metallic dragons had already suffered tremendous losses by the time dragon blood sorcery was discovered by humans, dragonborn of this type are somewhat of a rare sight, though more common than gem dragons.

Their bodies are coated in brilliant scales of gleaming metal, which shine in the sunlight. Some see them as living symbols of good and virtue, remnants of a bygone era when law and compassion ruled the world, though since no living creature can remember that age, these views are highly romanticized.

Gem

When the civil war began, a few chromatic dragons chased the dwarves of Stengrotto into the Underdark, tunneling deep into the earth and ever after living in gem-filled caverns in the deep. Over the course of thousands of years, they adapted to the psionic energy emitted by the gemstones, and were slowly transformed in a process similar to that which created the gem dwarves of Despin.

Knowledge of the gem dragons is scant even among the most learned of scholars, and, as such, gem dragonborn are incredibly rare. Unlike other dragons, gem dragons and gem dragonborn have no scales. Instead, their skin is hard, smooth, and polished. Broken pieces of their horns and bodies float diligently in their places due to their inherent psionic power.
