Genasi

Though the Wounded Isles are home to many a strange and alien creature, perhaps none are so otherworldly—yet, at the same time, so human—as the genasi. They are the children of genies: trickster elementals who frequently mingle with mortals and take great joy in meddling in mortal affairs. Though less common than elves, one is far more likely to encounter a genasi in any civilized area than, say, an aasimar or a tiefling. Though generally fun-loving and free-spirited, they are also dangerous, and carry the elemental powers of their outsider sires.

Subrace

Genasi have different traits depending on their heritage, resulting in four subraces: efreeti, djinn, marid, and dao. Choose one of the three subraces before creating your character. You gain all of the traits listed under the Aasimar Traits section, as well as those listed under your chosen subrace.

Genasi Traits

  • Age. Genasi mature at the same rate as humans, but can live up to 120 years.
  • Size. Genasi are similar to humans in height and build. Your size is Medium.
  • Speed.Your base walking speed is 30 feet.

Efreeti

Efreet (singular "efreeti") are the genies native to the plane of fire. While they hold honor in high regard, they are also cruel and unpredictable, and are very difficult to bind. As the children of efreet, these genasi are hotheaded and short-tempered, yet also keen of mind. They have a strong sense of pride in their identity, and are less likely to hide their otherworldly heritage from those who may persecute them. Efreeti genasi have skin the color of flames, coal, or ash, and their eyes flare when angered. Some have writhing, fiery red hair, while others sport actual flames atop their heads.

  • Ability Score Increase. Your Intelligence score increases by 2.
  • Blood of Fire. You have resistance to fire damage.
  • Reach to the Blaze. You know the Produce Flame cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you can cast the Burning Hands spell once as a 1st-level spell. You must finish a long rest to cast it again with this trait. Constitution is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

Djinn

  • Ability Score Increase. Your Dexterity score increases by 2.
  • Unending Breath. You can hold your breath indefinitely while you are not incapacitated.
  • Mingle With the Wind. You can cast the Levitate spell once, requiring no spell components. You must finish a short or long rest before you can cast it again with this trait.

Marid

  • Ability Score Increase. Your Wisdom score increases by 1.
  • Amphibious. You have a swim speed of 30 feet, and can breathe in water as well as air.
  • Call to the Wave. You know the Shape Water cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you can cast the Create or Destroy Water spell as a 2nd-level spell once. You must finish a short or long rest before you can cast it again with this trait. Constitution is your spellcasting ability for these spells.
